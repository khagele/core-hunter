Mesh-Hunter

A MeshCore node-hunting toolkit. Drive around with the RX webapp, and every reception lands on a shared coverage map — so you can home in on a node in the field.

Open the map Open the RX webapp

The map

The map at map.mesh-hunter.eu shows where MeshCore nodes have been heard. Without logging in you see recent, coarse coverage. Registered hunters see their own receptions in full detail; verified members see everything.

Log in on the map →

The RX webapp

The RX webapp at rx.mesh-hunter.eu turns a phone into a reception logger: Web Bluetooth to a MeshCore companion radio, plus the phone's GPS. Every packet it hears is timestamped, located, and published to the shared map.

It installs as a PWA — open it on your phone and add it to your home screen. Register inside the app to link your companion device and unlock your own data on the map.

Related projects

  • MeshCore — the mesh networking project this tool is built for.
  • CoreScope — the mesh observatory Mesh-Hunter builds on.

About & access

Mesh-Hunter is run by amateur-radio operators. Coverage is inferred from radio measurements via mesh topology — not from GPS tracking of any target node.

Verification to a member account (full map access) is granted by the admins. Register in the RX webapp to get a hunter account; ask an admin for member verification.
